Paper Presentation

Event

Participants are requested to present a ppt on any topic. Showcase your ideas in front of an esteemed panel of Judges. The presentation must be interesting, creative and deliver the Participants’ ideas within the time limit determined.

Procedure
  • Participants should prepare a PowerPoint presentation with a minimum of 5 slides.
  • Participants are requested to send their Presentation to sympocsbs@gmail.com before 23 Jan 2023.
  • The duration of the presentation must not exceed 3 minutes.
  • Plagiarism is strictly prohibited.
  • The juries will decide the winner.

General instructions
  • Time management will be strictly followed.
  • Candidates should send their ppt only to our Email ID on or before 23 Jan 2023.
  • The presentation should be in ‘ppt’ format.
  • Working models, Samples fetch you extra points.
  • A team can have 2-4 participants.
  • No participant can be a part of more than one team.

Following Details Should Be Submitted With The ppt
  • Title/Stream.
  • Name of the candidates (Team Members).
  • Department/Year/Section.
  • College name and address.
  • Contact Number and Email ID

Lost in tech

Event

An opportunity to think in other language. Come and work as a team to convert coding script into its counterpart. Then move ahead to stand a chance to win.

Round – 1: [Team]
  • Participants will be teamed up (3 participants).
  • 15 – 20 intermediate C/C++ programs will be given.
  • Participants will choose their program by lot process.
  • Plagiarism is strictly prohibited.
  • Fastest team completing will move to the next round. (Only first half will move to next round, other half will be eliminated)
  • Written code will be evaluated by the respective coordinator for best case, average case and worst case.

Round – 2: [Individual]
  • All participants will be given the same problem statement. [25-30 lines]
  • They should type their program with their screen off.
  • Time limit - 20 mins
  • Participants will be given 15 seconds to see their code when they wish [They cannot modify the code, only view the code], Only once.
  • One with no error [satisfies all test cases] or with the least number of errors [subject to test cases ]will be announced as the winner.

Shadow

Event

Invitation station to solve a mystery case. Find out the hidden clues and help demystify the conundrum in the table.

Procedure
  • The participants are provided with an investigation summary of the crime.
  • A group of 2 are allowed to participate as a team .
  • The event venue is frame-up with the visuals related to the events and some of the clues are also framed inside.
  • The participants are allowed to look after the visuals that are framed up inside the venue.
  • A maximum of 30 minutes are allocated for the participants to compete.
  • Participants are also provided with question papers, a notepad and a pen (for each team).
  • Participants are provided with highlights of the crime or suspicious incidents.
  • Question sets are given and the participants are supposed to have a discussion among the teammates.
  • The question set consists of clues of the suspicious incidents that occurred and some logical thinking abilities of the participants are needed to be applied here.
  • At last the participants have to make a discussion within themselves and they have to provide an investigation report to the event coordinators.
  • Scores are evaluated for each team at the end of the event.
  • The best investigation reports will be given the foremost importance by the event coordinators.
  • The winner and the runner will be announced and awarded based upon their scores and impression on their reports.

Bike Auction

Event

An sales event carried out with bidding points.

Procedure
  • A maximum of 3 members in a team are allowed to participate.
  • The event venue is frame-up with visuals related to the auction environment.
  • First of all, all the teams are provided with 100 bidding points.
  • The participants are supposed to answer the questions that are asked by the auctioneer (MCQs).
  • A maximum of 10 seconds are provided for the participants to answer each question.
  • Each team must answer every question that is being asked by the auctioneer.
  • For every correct answer, 5 bidding points will be added to the team's overall points and for every wrong answer, 10 bidding points will be deducted from the team's overall points.
  • After the completion of MCQ's round, teams are now provided with a bidding board.
  • The teams are provided with certain factors which they need to consider while bidding in the auction.
  • Then the auctioneer will start selling bikes in the auction.
  • Using the overall bidding points , the teams are allowed to bid for the bikes they're willing to buy.
  • After the completion of the auction round, each bike will be provided with certain points based on the factors which the auctioneer announced before the auction.
  • The winner and the runner will be announced and awarded based upon their points which they've earned in the auction table.
